Waratah anemone

By Noah Smith

The waratah anemone also known as actinia tenebrosa, is a sea anemone that is very common in of the eastern coast of Australia and New zealand. often found in crevices, cracks and overhang in rock pools in intertidal areas, when they are submerged under water it blooms and resembles a waratah which gives its name. They eat plankton. You will often find their young near them as their young come out of their mouth fully formed.
